A new garage door is one of the best returns on investment in home improvement — it can take up a third of your home’s street-facing façade, so the right choice makes a big visual impact. Here’s how to choose well.

## 1. Material

- **Steel** — the most popular: durable, low-maintenance, and available in many styles. Insulated steel is a great all-rounder.
- **Composite / wood-look** — the warmth of wood with far less upkeep.
- **Aluminum & glass** — sleek and modern, ideal for contemporary homes.

## 2. Insulation (R-value)

If your garage is attached, shares a wall with living space, or you use it as a workshop, insulation matters. A higher R-value keeps the garage warmer in our Illinois winters, cuts noise, and can lower energy bills. Look for a door with a solid polyurethane core.

## 3. Style and curb appeal

Match the door to your home’s architecture — carriage-house styles suit traditional homes, while flush or full-view doors suit modern ones. Window inserts add light and character. We’ll help you preview options before you commit.

## 4. Security and hardware

Modern doors come with stronger locking mechanisms and pair with smart openers for keyless, app-controlled access. If security is a priority, we’ll point you to the right features.

## 5. Budget — and financing

A quality door is an investment, but it shouldn’t mean paying everything up front. Ask us about **financing** so you can spread the cost and still get the door you actually want.
